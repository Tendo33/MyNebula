## Design Context

### Users
MyNebula is for people who star a lot of GitHub repositories, revisit them later, and want that collection to behave like a personal knowledge space instead of a flat bookmark list. They use it in focused research and exploration sessions, switching between scanning trends, filtering collections, and diving into relationships between repositories.

### Brand Personality
Precise, exploratory, calm.

The product should feel credible and thoughtfully engineered, but not sterile. It should support curiosity and long-form browsing without turning into a flashy sci-fi toy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first editorial utility with a subtle celestial tint.

The interface should feel like a well-kept research desk with a faint "nebula" atmosphere: soft ivory surfaces, slate typography, restrained indigo accents, and spatial depth that suggests exploration. Avoid generic admin SaaS chrome, harsh pure grays, and gimmicky space visuals. Keep the experience practical first, then distinctive in the details.

### Design Principles
1. Make exploration feel intentional: filters, panels, and tables should guide discovery rather than crowd it.
2. Keep the product calm under data density: typography, spacing, and color contrast should reduce cognitive load.
3. Build one coherent shell across Dashboard, Graph, Data, and Settings so the app reads as a single system.
4. Add personality through proportion, tone, and texture instead of decorative effects.
5. Treat polish as flagship quality: hover, focus, loading, empty, and disabled states must feel complete.

### Quality Bar
Flagship product polish for the current frontend shell and primary workflows.
